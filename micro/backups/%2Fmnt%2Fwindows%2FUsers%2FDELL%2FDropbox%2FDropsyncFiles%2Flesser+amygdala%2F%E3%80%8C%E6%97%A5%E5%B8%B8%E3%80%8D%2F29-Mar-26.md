# **10. LRU Approximation in Linux**

*(Main “implementation” section — highest marks)*

Break into subparts:

### 10.1 Basic Idea

* Linux uses **approximation of LRU**
* Based on “recently used vs not recently used”

---

### 10.2 Active and Inactive Lists

* Two lists:

  * **Active list** → recently used pages
  * **Inactive list** → less used pages
* Pages move between lists based on usage

📌 *(Diagram: “Two lists: Active ↔ Inactive with arrows showing movement of pages”)*

---

### 10.3 Reference Bit Mechanism

* Each page has a **reference bit**
* If accessed → marked “used”
* OS periodically checks and updates lists

---

### 10.4 Page Reclamation

* Replacement happens from:

  * **Inactive list first**
* If still needed → move back to active

📌 *(Diagram: “Page reclaim process from inactive list → disk”)*

---

### 10.5 Advantages of Approximation

* Less overhead than pure LRU
* Works well with real workloads
* Scalable for large systems
